# NPK Blends

> Complete fertilizers that deliver the three primary nutrients — nitrogen (N),
> phosphorus (P) and potassium (K) — together, in ratios matched to the crop and
> soil.

## What it is

NPK products supply a plant's three main nutrients in a single application. The
three numbers on every grade — for example **15-15-15** or **20-10-10** — give
the percentage of N, P₂O₅ and K₂O. Getting that ratio right for the crop stage
and the soil's existing fertility is what separates a good yield from a wasted
one, which is why NPK is sold in a wide range of formulas rather than a single
recipe.

## Key applications

- **Balanced crop nutrition** — cereals, fruits, vegetables, plantations and turf.
- **Stage-specific feeding** — higher-N grades for growth, higher-P for
  establishment and rooting, higher-K for fruiting and quality.
- **Soil-corrective programmes** — formulas chosen to complement what the soil
  already provides.

## Grades & types

- **Compound NPK** — every granule contains all three nutrients, giving even
  distribution across the field.
- **Bulk blends** — separate nutrient granules physically mixed to a target
  ratio; flexible and cost-effective.
- **With micronutrients** — sulphur, zinc, boron and others added for specific
  deficiencies.
- Key checks: the **N-P₂O₅-K₂O grade, granule size/consistency, water solubility**
  and **chloride vs sulphate** potassium source.

## Handling, storage & logistics

Supplied **bagged or in bulk**, with anti-caking treatment and dry storage to
keep granules free-flowing. Blends are made up to the customer's target ratio,
so accurate formulation and consistent granulometry are central to quality.

## What buyers look for

The **ratio suited to their crop and soil**, uniform granules for even
spreading, the right potassium source (chloride-tolerant crop or not), and any
micronutrients their programme needs — delivered on time for the application
window.
