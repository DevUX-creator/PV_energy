# Ammonium Sulfate

> A dual-nutrient fertilizer that supplies nitrogen **and** sulphur together —
> graded around **21% N and 24% S** — for crops and soils that need both.

## What it is

Ammonium sulfate delivers readily available nitrogen alongside sulphur, a
nutrient many soils are increasingly short of as cleaner air has cut atmospheric
sulphur deposition. It also has a mild acidifying effect, which makes it a
natural fit for alkaline soils. Much of the world's supply is produced as a
co-product of the caprolactam and steel industries, and it's valued both as a
standalone fertilizer and as a quality blending ingredient.

## Key applications

- **Sulphur-hungry crops** — oilseeds, brassicas, onions, tea and forage
  respond strongly to the sulphur.
- **Alkaline soils** — the acidifying effect helps free up nutrients in
  high-pH ground.
- **Blending component** — a clean, uniform source of N and S in NPK and
  bulk-blend formulas.

## Grades & forms

- **Crystalline, granular and compacted** forms — granular and compacted grades
  suit bulk blending and mechanical spreading; crystalline suits solution and
  industrial use.
- Key checks: **nitrogen ≈21%, sulphur ≈24%, moisture and granulometry**, plus
  the source grade (**caprolactam vs synthetic**).

## Handling, storage & logistics

Supplied **bagged or in bulk**, stored dry to stay free-flowing. Granular grades
are prized in blending because their size and density pair well with urea, DAP
and potash without segregating in the bag.

## What buyers look for

The **form** suited to their use (granular for spreading and blending,
crystalline for solution), reliable **N and S content**, consistent particle
size for even blends, and dependable supply — especially where sulphur is the
limiting nutrient.
