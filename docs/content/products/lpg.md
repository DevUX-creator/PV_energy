# LPG (Liquefied Petroleum Gas)

> A portable, clean-burning fuel — propane, butane, or a blend of the two — kept
> liquid under modest pressure so a large amount of energy travels in a small,
> transportable volume.

## What it is

LPG is recovered as a by-product of natural-gas processing and crude-oil
refining. At normal temperature it turns to liquid under light pressure and
returns to gas when released, which makes it easy to store in cylinders and
tanks and to move where pipelines don't reach. It burns cleanly — virtually
sulphur-free and sootless — which is why demand keeps growing wherever operators
want a lower-emission alternative to diesel, coal or wood.

## Key applications

- **Residential & commercial** — cooking, space and water heating, and reliable
  off-grid energy for homes, restaurants and remote sites.
- **Autogas** — a lower-emission transport fuel for converted petrol engines,
  widely used in taxi and delivery fleets.
- **Industrial** — clean process heat for kilns, dryers and furnaces, plus
  feedstock for petrochemicals such as propylene.
- **Agriculture** — crop and grain drying, greenhouse and poultry-shed heating.
- **Aerosols & refrigeration** — propellant and CFC-free refrigerant use.

## Grades & specifications

- **Commercial propane (C₃)** — higher vapour pressure; performs in cold
  climates and heavier industrial duty.
- **Commercial butane (C₄)** — lower vapour pressure; common for indoor and
  warmer-climate use.
- **Propane–butane mixes** — blended to a target vapour pressure, often adjusted
  seasonally (more propane in winter).
- Traded against recognised standards (e.g. **EN 589** for autogas, **GPA/ASTM**
  methods). Buyers typically check **C₃/C₄ ratio, vapour pressure, sulphur and
  moisture**.

## Handling, storage & logistics

Shipped and stored as a pressurised (or, at scale, refrigerated) liquid — in
cylinders, bulk road and rail tankers, and by sea in gas carriers (VLGCs for
long-haul). LPG is heavier than air and disperses slowly, so pressurised
containment and correct venting are core to safe handling end-to-end.

## What buyers look for

Delivery mode (cylinder, bulk or autogas), the C₃/C₄ specification suited to the
climate and application, and consistent quality across cargoes — plus dependable
scheduling, which for a fuel this seasonal is often as important as price.
