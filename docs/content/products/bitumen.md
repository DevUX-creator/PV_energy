# Bitumen

> The heavy binder at the bottom of the barrel — the material that holds asphalt
> together and waterproofs the built environment.

## What it is

Bitumen is the dense, sticky residue that remains after the lightest and
middle fractions are distilled from crude. Blended with aggregate it becomes
asphalt — the surface of most of the world's roads, runways and hard-standing —
and on its own it's a durable waterproofing and sealing material. Demand tracks
infrastructure spending, so it's a product closely tied to construction cycles
in each region.

## Key applications

- **Road construction & paving** — the binder in asphalt concrete for highways,
  streets, airports and industrial yards.
- **Waterproofing & roofing** — membranes, felts and coatings that keep water
  out of buildings and structures.
- **Industrial uses** — pipe coatings, sealants, paints and specialist
  protective linings.

## Grades & specifications

- **Penetration grades** — e.g. **60/70, 80/100** — classified by how far a
  standard needle penetrates the sample; chosen to suit the local climate.
- **Viscosity grades (VG-10 to VG-40)** — graded by flow behaviour at working
  temperature.
- **Performance grades (PG)** and **polymer-modified bitumen (PMB)** — engineered
  for heavier traffic and temperature extremes.
- **Oxidised/industrial grades** — harder grades for roofing and industrial use.
  Key checks: **penetration, softening point, ductility and viscosity**.

## Handling, storage & logistics

Moved and stored **hot** to stay liquid — in insulated/heated bitumen tankers,
rail and road tankers, and heated shore tanks — or supplied **packed** in drums,
bulk bags and heat-resistant "bitubags" for markets without hot infrastructure.
Emulsions and cutback grades extend where and how it can be applied.

## What buyers look for

The grade matched to the climate and specification of the job, consistent
penetration and softening point across deliveries, and a supply format
(hot bulk vs packed) that fits the receiving site's handling capability.
