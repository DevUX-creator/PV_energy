# DAP (Diammonium Phosphate)

> The world's most widely used phosphate fertilizer — a concentrated source of
> phosphorus with a useful dose of nitrogen, graded **18-46-0**.

## What it is

DAP is made by reacting ammonia with phosphoric acid, producing a granule that
carries **18% nitrogen and 46% phosphate (P₂O₅)**. Phosphorus drives early root
development and energy transfer in plants, and DAP's high concentration and good
handling make it the go-to phosphate source at planting across most of the
world's cropping systems.

## Key applications

- **Crop establishment** — placed at or before sowing to build strong roots and
  early vigour.
- **Broad-acre nutrition** — a staple phosphate source for cereals, oilseeds,
  pulses and more.
- **Blending component** — a phosphate base combined with other products into
  custom NPK programmes.

## Grades & specifications

- Standard grade **18-46-0** (18% N, 46% P₂O₅).
- Key checks: **nutrient content, granule size and moisture**, plus **cadmium
  content**, which some import markets regulate.
- Often compared with **MAP (11-52-0)** — MAP is more acidic and higher in
  phosphate; DAP carries more nitrogen and suits a wider range of soils.

## Handling, storage & logistics

Supplied **bulk or bagged**, with dry storage to prevent caking. Its firm,
uniform granules handle, blend and spread well, which is part of why it moves so
widely in international trade.

## What buyers look for

**DAP vs MAP** for their soil and crop, consistent granulometry for blending and
spreading, low moisture, and — for regulated destinations — compliant cadmium
levels, backed by reliable delivery into the planting window.
