# Urea

> The world's most widely used solid nitrogen fertilizer — the highest nitrogen
> content of any dry fertilizer, at around 46% N.

## What it is

Urea is made by combining ammonia with carbon dioxide, producing a
nitrogen-rich solid that dissolves readily and feeds crops efficiently. Its high
concentration means more nutrient per tonne shipped and spread, which lowers
freight and handling cost per unit of nitrogen — a big reason it dominates
global nitrogen trade. Beyond the field, a purer "technical" grade serves
industry and emissions control.

## Key applications

- **Broad-acre agriculture** — the primary nitrogen source for cereals, rice,
  maize, sugarcane and more.
- **Foliar & fertigation** — dissolved for spray or drip application.
- **Technical / industrial** — feedstock for resins, adhesives and melamine.
- **Emissions control (DEF/AdBlue)** — high-purity urea solution that cuts NOₓ
  from diesel engines.
- **Animal feed** — a controlled non-protein nitrogen source for ruminants.

## Grades & specifications

- **Granular** — larger, harder granules suited to bulk blending and mechanical
  spreading.
- **Prilled** — smaller prills, common for direct application and technical use.
- Key checks: **nitrogen ≈46%, biuret content** (kept low for foliar use),
  **moisture and granulometry** (size consistency for even spreading).

## Handling, storage & logistics

Supplied **bagged** (50 kg or one-tonne jumbo bags) or in **bulk**. Urea is
hygroscopic — it draws moisture from the air — so dry storage, good bagging and
anti-caking treatment protect quality from plant to field.

## What buyers look for

**Granular vs prilled** for their application method, **low biuret** where the
crop is sensitive, consistent granule size for even spreading, and moisture-safe
packaging — plus steady supply through the planting season.
