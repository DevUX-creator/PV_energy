# Ammonia

> The starting point for nearly all nitrogen fertilizers and a major industrial
> chemical — and, increasingly, a carrier for clean hydrogen energy.

## What it is

Ammonia (NH₃) is produced by combining nitrogen from the air with hydrogen,
mostly from natural gas. At around 82% nitrogen it's the most concentrated
nitrogen product of all, and it's the feedstock from which urea, ammonium
nitrate, DAP and other fertilizers are made. It's also a workhorse industrial
chemical — and attention is growing fast around its role as a zero-carbon fuel
and a practical way to move hydrogen around the world.

## Key applications

- **Fertilizer feedstock** — the base for urea, ammonium nitrate, phosphates and
  more.
- **Direct field application** — anhydrous ammonia injected straight into soil
  as a high-efficiency nitrogen source.
- **Industrial chemistry** — explosives, plastics, fibres, pharmaceuticals and
  refrigeration (refrigerant R717).
- **Emissions control** — reagent in selective catalytic reduction (SCR) to cut
  NOₓ.
- **Emerging clean energy** — a hydrogen carrier and low-carbon fuel under active
  development.

## Grades & specifications

- **Anhydrous ammonia** in commercial, refrigeration and metallurgical grades,
  distinguished by **purity and water content**.
- Key checks: **NH₃ purity, moisture/water content and oil content**, matched to
  the end use.

## Handling, storage & logistics

Handled as a liquefied gas — either **refrigerated at about −33 °C** or **stored
under pressure** — in specialised, insulated gas carriers and tanks. It's toxic
and requires strict safety, containment and trained handling at every step, so
disciplined logistics are as important as the product itself.

## What buyers look for

The **purity grade** for their process, low water/oil content, and above all a
supplier with the safety systems, certified equipment and experience to move a
hazardous product reliably and responsibly.
