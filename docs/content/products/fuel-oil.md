# Fuel Oil

> The heavy, residual fuels that power ships, plants and heavy industry —
> supplied across the sulphur grades the market now demands.

## What it is

Fuel oil is the dense residue left after lighter products are distilled from
crude. It carries a lot of energy per barrel at a competitive price, which is
why it remains the workhorse fuel for marine engines, power stations and large
industrial boilers. Since **IMO 2020** capped marine sulphur, the market has
split clearly into low- and high-sulphur grades, and matching the right grade to
the right engine or scrubber setup is now central to every deal.

## Key applications

- **Marine bunkering** — the primary fuel for deep-sea shipping, delivered
  ship-to-ship or at berth.
- **Power generation** — feeding utility and captive power plants, especially
  where gas isn't available.
- **Industrial heat** — firing boilers, furnaces and kilns in heavy industry.
- **Refinery feedstock** — reprocessed into lighter products where upgrading
  capacity exists.

## Grades & specifications

- **VLSFO (≤0.5% sulphur)** — the post-IMO-2020 standard marine grade.
- **ULSFO (≤0.1% sulphur)** — for Emission Control Areas (ECAs).
- **HSFO (≈3.5% sulphur)** — for vessels fitted with exhaust scrubbers, and for
  permitted industrial use.
- **Bunker grades to ISO 8217** — e.g. RMG 380, RMK 500 — defined by
  **viscosity (cSt at 50 °C), density, flash point, pour point, CCAI and
  water/sediment**.

## Handling, storage & logistics

Stored and pumped **heated** to stay fluid, held in dedicated tankage, and
delivered by bunker barge or pipeline. Stability and compatibility between
blends matter — mixing incompatible fuels can drop out sludge, so provenance and
testing are part of responsible supply.

## What buyers look for

The correct sulphur grade for their compliance setup, an on-spec **ISO 8217**
result, viscosity that suits their equipment, and assurance of stability — plus
dependable barge scheduling at the load or bunker port.
