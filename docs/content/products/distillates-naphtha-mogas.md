# Distillates: Naphtha & Mogas

> Light distillate streams that move easily between two worlds — petrochemical
> feedstock and finished transport fuel — giving buyers flexibility across
> markets and seasons.

## What they are

**Naphtha** and **mogas (motor gasoline)** come off the lighter end of the
refining process. Naphtha is a versatile intermediate: it feeds petrochemical
plants and blends into gasoline. Mogas is the finished road fuel that reaches
service stations. Because these grades sit at the crossover of chemicals and
fuels, they're among the most actively traded and arbitraged products in the
barrel.

## Key applications

- **Naphtha as feedstock** — cracked to make ethylene and propylene (the base of
  most plastics) and reformed into aromatics and high-octane gasoline
  components.
- **Mogas as transport fuel** — on-spec petrol for cars and light vehicles,
  supplied to regional octane and emissions standards.
- **Gasoline blending** — naphtha and blendstocks combined to hit octane,
  volatility and sulphur targets for each destination.
- **Solvent & industrial use** — selected naphtha cuts used as industrial
  solvents.

## Grades & specifications

- **Light vs heavy naphtha** — split by boiling range; light naphtha favours
  steam cracking, heavy naphtha favours reforming. Paraffin/naphthene/aromatic
  (**P/N/A**) content, density and sulphur define the grade.
- **Mogas octane grades** — typically **RON 91 / 95 / 98**, with ethanol or
  other oxygenate blends where mandated.
- Key specs buyers check: **octane (RON/MON), Reid vapour pressure (RVP,
  adjusted seasonally), sulphur, and oxygenate content** — all vary by market
  (e.g. Euro-grade sulphur limits).

## Handling, storage & logistics

Moved as **clean petroleum products (CPP)** on product tankers and through
dedicated shore tankage, with blending at hubs to meet each market's
specification. Kept segregated from heavier, dirtier grades to protect quality.

## What buyers look for

The right octane and volatility for the destination and season, a sulphur spec
that clears local regulations, and reliable delivery into the hub or refinery
that needs it — flexibility across grades is the main value here.
