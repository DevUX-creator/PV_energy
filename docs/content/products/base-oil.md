# Base Oil

> The refined oil that makes up most of every lubricant — the base that
> additives are blended into to create engine oils, greases and industrial
> fluids.

## What it is

Base oil is a highly refined petroleum (or synthetic) stock that forms the bulk
of a finished lubricant, typically 70–90% by volume. Its quality — how well it
resists heat, oxidation and thickening — sets the ceiling for the lubricant made
from it. The industry classifies base oils into **API Groups**, and the higher
groups command a premium because they deliver cleaner, more stable, longer-life
oils.

## Key applications

- **Automotive lubricants** — engine oils, transmission and gear oils.
- **Industrial lubricants** — hydraulic, turbine, compressor and circulating
  oils.
- **Greases & metalworking fluids** — the fluid base for a wide range of
  specialty products.
- **Process & transformer oils** — electrical insulation and industrial process
  applications.

## Grades & specifications

- **Group I** — solvent-refined; the traditional, lower-cost base stock.
- **Group II** — hydrotreated; clearer, more stable, now the mainstream choice.
- **Group III** — severely hydrocracked; very high viscosity index, often
  marketed as synthetic-grade.
- **Group IV (PAO)** and **Group V (esters and others)** — true synthetics for
  demanding performance.
- Common viscosity cuts are quoted as **Solvent Neutral grades — SN 150, SN 500,
  Bright Stock** — with **viscosity index, pour point, flash point and sulphur**
  the key quality markers.

## Handling, storage & logistics

Shipped in **flexitanks, ISO tanks, drums or bulk parcel tankers**, and kept
clean and segregated by grade to avoid cross-contamination — critical, because
blenders rely on consistent, on-spec stock to hit their own formulations.

## What buyers look for

The **API group and viscosity grade** their formulation calls for, a strong
**viscosity index**, and batch-to-batch consistency — a blender's finished-oil
approvals depend on the base oil arriving exactly to specification.
